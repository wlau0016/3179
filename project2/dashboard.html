About Japan Tourism

Japan is the most amazing tourist destination and it offers many unique experiences that you cannot find in any other part of the world. The culture of this country is an interesting blend of Eastern traditions and Western modernity that can be seen everywhere. Japan is one of the oldest civilizations and has a beautiful and diverse history. The stunning, diverse scenery with mountains and breathtaking views, which are much appreciated by the Japanese, offers so many different experiences that attract tourists from all corners of the world. In this dashboard, it gives you the visualisations in tourism for your reference to make an interesting and enjoyable trip to this amazing country at least once in your lifetime.

Visitor Arrivals to Japan By Country

From the map visualisation, most of the visitors come from South East Asia. Vietnamese contribute to the largest number of visitors, 104,860. In contrast, except for United States, fewer visitors from the European countries visit Japan in 2022. Visit Rate of Prefecture

Top 5 Visit Rate of Prefecture

Tokyo

Tokyo have a lot of tourist spots like Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden, Meiji Jingu Shrine, Asakusa. Furthermore, you can also enjoy walking on street of Shibuya, Harajuku and Ginza to see latest trend fashion.

Osaka

Osaka is the secondary biggest metropolitan area in Japan. We have a lot of must-see spots in Osaka; for example, Osaka Castle, Dotonbori, Shinsekai, Umeda , Shinsaibashi, Bay area and so on.

Chiba

Chiba Prefecture is located east of Tokyo. A large part of the prefecture is made up of the rural Boso Peninsula which is a popular resort destinations for Tokyoites.

Kyoto

There are a lot of Shrines & Temples like Kiyomizu-dera, Kinkakuji, Fushimi Inari-taisya Shrine at Kyoto. In addition, it's also have places which are knowed as Japan Travel Symbol like Arashiyama, Gyon.

Nara

Nara is a landlocked prefecture in the Kansai Region, is the cradle of the Japanese civilization. Many of the early emperors built their palaces in Nara Prefecture, formerly known as Yamato.
